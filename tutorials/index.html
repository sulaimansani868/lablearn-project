Laboratory Tutorials

Practical guides built from ten years of bench experience — from foundational concepts to advanced analytical techniques. All free.

Every tutorial in this library is written from the bench up — grounded in real laboratory work rather than textbook abstractions. Whether you are calibrating a balance for the first time or validating an HPLC method against ICH Q2(R1) guidelines, you will find step-by-step guidance, underlying theory, common pitfalls, and safety considerations in one place. The collection spans analytical chemistry, biochemistry, clinical laboratory science, and molecular biology, and is structured so that each tutorial can stand alone or serve as part of a deliberate learning progression.

Beginner
Core Laboratory Skills

Designed for students entering the laboratory for the first time and practitioners looking to formalise foundational knowledge. Topics cover essential calculations (molarity, serial dilutions, molar solutions), safe handling of chemicals and equipment, correct use of balances, micropipettes, and centrifuges, and basic technique for sample preparation. No prior laboratory experience is assumed.

Intermediate
Analytical & Clinical Methods

Intended for students and scientists who are comfortable with basic technique and ready to engage with instrumental methods and quantitative analysis. Covers UV-Vis spectrophotometry, titration, buffer preparation, standard solutions, blood glucose and protein determination, enzyme activity assays, Kjeldahl analysis, reagent-grade water, and proper biological sample handling. Assumes familiarity with Beginner-level concepts.

Advanced
Specialised Techniques & Validation

Aimed at senior students, researchers, and laboratory professionals working with sophisticated instrumentation or regulatory frameworks. Topics include HPLC method development and ICH validation, atomic absorption spectroscopy, enzyme kinetics (Michaelis-Menten, inhibition), SDS-PAGE and agarose gel electrophoresis, spectrofluorimetry, and ELISA immunoassay formats. A solid grounding in Intermediate methods is recommended.
