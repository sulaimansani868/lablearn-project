Background

My name is Sani Sulaiman. I am a Senior Laboratory Technician in the Department of Biochemistry at Sa’adu Zungur University, Gadau, Bauchi State, Nigeria, where I have worked since 2012. Over more than a decade at the bench I have been involved in undergraduate teaching laboratories, analytical chemistry demonstrations, biochemical analysis, and supporting student practical work across multiple courses.

LabLearn grew directly out of that experience. I noticed that students frequently struggled not because they lacked intelligence, but because the resources available to them — textbooks, printed lab manuals, and photocopied handouts — were difficult to access, often out of date, and rarely written the way a practising laboratory scientist would explain things at the bench. I wanted to build something different: free, practical, and written from real experience rather than reformatted theory.

Everything on this platform — every tutorial, calculator, and tool — is built and maintained by me alone, alongside my full-time laboratory responsibilities. If it has helped your studies or your work, that is the only reward this project needs.

Why LabLearn exists

Four principles behind the platform

🔓

Free forever

Good laboratory education should not cost money. Every tutorial, calculator, and app will always be free — no paywalls, no subscriptions, no registration.

🔬

Experience-first

Everything comes from real laboratory work at Sa’adu Zungur University. Not reformatted textbook content — practical knowledge from the bench.

📐

Precision matters

Laboratory errors have consequences. Every formula, procedure, and calculation tool is checked carefully to the standard expected in professional analytical work.

🌍

Accessible everywhere

All apps work offline. All tools run in the browser. No account, no internet connection required — whether you are in a university lab or a rural health centre.
